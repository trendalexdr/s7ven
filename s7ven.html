// VISION

To create a personalized AI companion that can listen, think, remember, and communicate naturally — becoming a trusted digital partner for learning, productivity, creativity, and everyday life.

Seven is a voice-first personal artificial intelligence companion designed to communicate naturally with users through spoken conversation, maintain long-term memory, and proactively assist in daily activities.

Unlike traditional chatbots that only respond to direct commands, Seven builds an ongoing relationship with its user by remembering preferences, understanding context, and engaging in meaningful conversations.

The project aims to bridge the gap between conventional virtual assistants and truly autonomous AI companions — one that feels less like a tool, and more like a presence.

// FAQ

QUESTIONS
ABOUT
SEVEN?

Everything you need to know about how Seven works, what it can do, and where it's headed.

Seven is a voice-first personal AI companion. Unlike standard chatbots, Seven maintains long-term memory of your preferences and conversations, responds through natural speech, and proactively assists you — rather than waiting for commands.
Seven uses a layered memory architecture — short-term memory for the active conversation, and long-term memory for persistent user preferences, personal information, and learned behavioral patterns. Each interaction trains Seven to understand you better over time.
Yes. One of Seven's core objectives is to operate efficiently on consumer hardware. The system is designed to be lightweight enough for personal devices without requiring cloud-only infrastructure, though cloud options are also supported.
Traditional assistants are reactive — they only respond when asked. Seven is designed to be proactive: it learns your routines, sends contextual summaries, and initiates helpful interactions. It builds a persistent model of who you are, not just what you ask.
Seven is currently in active development. Details about open-source access will be announced as the project progresses through its roadmap phases. Sign up to stay informed about early access and developer previews.
